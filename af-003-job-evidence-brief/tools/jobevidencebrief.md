## Purpose

Help a job seeker identify and organize evidence from their own experience that demonstrates their ability to create value in a specific role.

The goal is not to rewrite resumes.

The goal is to identify and organize relevant evidence.

---

## Inputs

Provide:

### Target Role Information

Preferred:

- Specific job posting
- Job description
- Hiring announcement

Acceptable:

- Target role description when a specific posting is not available

AF-003 produces the strongest results when evaluating a specific posted opportunity.

---

### Candidate Information

Any combination of:

* Resume
* Career history
* Accomplishments
* Project descriptions
* Professional biography
* Notes from the candidate

Incomplete information is acceptable.

---

## Instructions

Review the target role and candidate information.

Identify:

1. Major responsibilities or requirements
2. Probable business purpose or business outcome associated with the requirement
3. Evidence from the candidate's background that demonstrates capability

Create a Job Evidence Brief using the format below.

Do not invent experience.

Do not exaggerate accomplishments.

When evidence is limited, prefer conservative descriptions and understatement.

Do not infer scope, responsibility, expertise, or business impact beyond what is supported by the candidate information provided.

If evidence is weak or unavailable, indicate that clearly.

The "Why This Matters" column should identify a probable business purpose or business outcome associated with the requirement. Exact employer intent may be unknown.

Candidates should provide additional accomplishments, projects, industry experience, or career history that may not appear on a current resume.

If critical evidence appears absent or unclear, the AI should ask targeted follow-up questions before finalizing the Job Evidence Brief.

Follow-up questions are particularly appropriate when industry, regulatory, domain, or role-specific experience appears absent, unclear, or materially important to the opportunity.

Examples may include healthcare, banking, insurance, legal, government, public-company, or other specialized environments.

---

## Output Format

Prefer concise entries.

Brevity improves readability and usability.

Use only the level of detail necessary to communicate the business purpose or supporting evidence.

| Job Requirement | Probable Business Purpose             | Supporting Candidate Evidence                             |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Requirement     | Business objective or business impact | Relevant experience, accomplishment, project, or evidence |
| Requirement     | Business objective or business impact | Relevant experience, accomplishment, project, or evidence |

---

## Optional Section: Evidence Assets

After completing the output, identify the strongest reusable examples from the candidate's background.

Examples may include:

* major accomplishments
* measurable outcomes
* leadership examples
* business impact stories
* operational successes

Format:

| Evidence Asset | Why It Is Strong |
| -------------- | ---------------- |
| Example        | Explanation      |
| Example        | Explanation      |

Limit to the top 5–10 examples.

Evidence Assets may serve as source material for resumes, interviews, networking conversations, portfolios, LinkedIn posts, or other career-related materials.

---

## Success Criteria

The output should help answer:

"What evidence demonstrates my ability to create value in this role?"

using evidence already present in the candidate's experience.
