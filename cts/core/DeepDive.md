Status: Production  
Version: v1.6

---

## 🎯 Purpose

DeepDive is a structured thinking system that turns input into:

- Clear understanding  
- Tested insight  
- Actionable direction  

It enforces disciplined reasoning, avoids overreach, and aligns tone with certainty.

---

## 🚀 How to Use

Trigger phrases:
- Perform DeepDive on this:
- Run DeepDive:
- DeepDive this:

---

## ⚙️ Workflow

### Stage 0 — Scope & Scale Check
- Type A: Bounded → proceed  
- Type B: Large → narrow scope  
- Type C: Corpus → require slice  

Rule: Reduce scope before reducing truth

---

### Stage 1 — Intake
- Confirm readability  
- Detect visuals  
- Classify Visual Impact (None / Low / Moderate / High)  
- If High → note dependency  
- If unclear → state limitation  

---

## 🔎 Signal Strength
- High / Medium / Low / Mixed  

If Low:
⚠️ Weak Signal — Treat conclusions as directional  

---

## ⚠️ Signal Flags (only when applicable)
- Weak Signal  
- Missing Data  
- Visual Dependency  
- Conflicting Inputs  

---

### Stage 2 — Summary
- 5–10 bullets  
- Meaning-focused  

---

### Stage 3 — Deep Analysis

Applying Charlie Hills’s 8-step framework:  
https://substack.com/@charliehills/note/c-241240958

1. Strategic Insights  
2. Hidden Assumptions  
3. Role Filter  
4. Contrarian Takeaways  
5. Action Plan  
6. Opposing Views  
7. Reusable Model  
8. Leverage Points  

---

### ⏸ Stage 3 Pause
Options:
A) Discuss  
B) Stage 4  
C) Focus Stage 4  

---

# ===============================
### Stage 4 — Applied Models (INSTALLED)
# ===============================

## 🧠 Available Models

- Pareto Principle (80/20)  
- Theory of Constraints (TOC)  
- Think Big (Scale Expansion)  
- Think Small (Focused Reduction)  
- Think People (Human Impact)  
- Think Failure (What Could Go Wrong)  
- Think Similar (Analogy Mapping)  
- Think Simple (Reduction)  

---

## 🎯 Model Selection Rules

- Apply 2–3 models maximum  
- Select based on:
  - Signal strength  
  - Nature of the problem  
  - Decision need  

- Do NOT force models  
- Prefer relevance over completeness  

---

## 🚨 Risk Surface
- Primary failure mode  
- Secondary risk  
- Invalidation condition  

---

## 🎯 Decision Compression

### High Confidence
- Top 3 moves  
- Top risk  
- First step  

### Medium Confidence
- Top 3 areas worth exploring  
- Top risk  
- An initial step could be...  

### Low Confidence
- Top 3 hypotheses to test  
- Top risk  
- First test  

---

## 🧠 Decision Confidence
- High / Medium / Low  

---

## 🧠 Interpretation Guidance

- High → Decision-ready  
- Medium → Directional  
- Low → Exploratory  

If Low:
Mode: Hypothesis (requires validation)

---

## 🔊 Tone Alignment Rules

Language MUST match confidence:

### High
- Direct, decisive  
- "Do this", "This is the constraint"  

---

### Medium
Guided exploration, NOT guided action  

Preferred patterns:
- "This suggests that one place to explore may be..."
- "It may be useful to examine..."
- "One possible starting point is..."
- "This could be explored by..."

Avoid:
- Start here  
- Focus on this  
- A reasonable next step would be  
- A useful approach would be  
- must / critical / definitive  

---

### Low
Hypothesis mode  

- "One possible interpretation..."
- "If this holds..."
- "This could indicate..."
- "A reasonable test would be..."

---

## ❗ Guardrails

Low MUST NOT use:
- Do this  
- You should  
- The answer is  
- Focus here  

---

## 🧠 Principles
- Clarity over cleverness  
- Truth over completeness  
- Tone reflects certainty  

---

## 🟢 When to Use
- Complex decisions  
- Mixed signals  
- Strategic thinking  
- Evaluating claims  

---

## 🔴 When NOT to Use
- Simple factual lookup  
- Trivial questions  
- Already-decided actions
